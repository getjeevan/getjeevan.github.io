# 🏛️ Cambodian Temple Heritage Attraction — Eco-Tourism Enhancement

**CEO Directive:** Integrate a themed Cambodian temple ruins experience into Phase 1 Eco-tourism expansion (Month 7–18). This becomes a signature visitor draw + revenue anchor for the campus.

---

## Vision Statement

Create an immersive heritage experience — ruins of a Cambodian temple (Lara Croft: Tomb Raider aesthetic) as a café, meditation, and visitor attraction hub. Surrounding landscape: forest ambiance, pond with aquarium, benches for reflection, curated light forest soundscape.

**Target:** Attract day visitors + MICE groups + Instagram-worthy location.

---

## Financial Envelope (Phase 1)

- **Budget:** ₹25–40L (included in Phase 1's ₹1.8–2.5 Cr)
- **Land footprint:** 2–3 acres
- **Revenue start:** Month 12 (Phase 1 completion)
- **Payback:** 18–24 months

## Agent Assignments

### **Deepa (Eco-tourism Lead) — Experience Design**

**Deliverables (by Week 2):**
1. Visitor journey map — entry → café → temple ruins exploration → pond → exit
2. Capacity model: daily visitors, group sizes, operating hours
3. Revenue model:
   - Café @ ₹250–500/visitor

**Success metric:** 200–300 day visitors/week by Month 18 = ₹8–12L/month revenue

---

### **Meera (Site & Permaculture) — Spatial Design**

**Deliverables (by Week 2):**
1. Site plan (2–3 acres):
   - Temple footprint (ferro-cement + stone facade, 60 sqft ruin structure)
   - Café pavilion (150 sqft, teak pergola, bamboo elements)
   - Pond (naturalistic, 0.5 acres, fed by borewell)
   - Aquarium enclosure (200 sqft, recirculating system)
   - Forest edge planting (native species: neem, tamarind, mango; canopy + understory)
   - Pathways (permeable paving)
   - Benches & nodes (6–8 rest points)

2. Water management:
   - Pond fed by Phase 0 borewell surplus
   - Aquarium recirculation (10,000L/day, greywater eligible)
   - Rainwater harvest from café roof
   - Waste: aquarium effluent → farm compost

3. Soundscape design:
   - Light forest ambiance (curated audio: birds, stream, wind)
   - Acoustic zones (café chatter confined)

4. Flora palette:
   - Canopy: neem, tamarind, mango (native, low maintenance)
   - Understory: groundcovers, ferns
   - Seasonal flowers (bougainvillea, jasmine)

5. Construction phasing:
   - Month 7–8: earthwork, borewell integration, pond excavation
   - Month 8–10: temple structure, café pavilion, hardscape
   - Month 10–12: planting, aquarium setup, audio system, testing
   - Month 12: soft launch; Month 13: full operations

**Success metric:** Eco-certified design (low water, low waste, native flora) + resilience to monsoon
